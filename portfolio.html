Descriptions and Process

Overview:

Master of Digital Media (MDM) program at the Center for Digital media is a special program that aims to improve communication skills of students and professionals that are in creative industries through improvisation. We are focusing on this special method to improve communication skills and its lecturer/designer Dr. Pennefather. Our goal is presenting the effectiveness of this class and specifically, Ball Game and other games help students to improve their team building and communication skills. And how these games prepare students to meet UX clients. The primary audience is students and professionals who will be inspired by the program and specifically the games. We want to express improvisation is a fun and great way to improve their communication skills in the creative industry.

Project problems:

The beginning of storyboard is interesting but missing some connecting scenes between lecture and games.The colors scheme is stated and there are pictures of the exterior of Center for Digital media as establishing shots. I have made a dramatic change due to some issues regarding in-class footage so even though preproduction have provided a general guideline and it has explained what the main point of interest of what we want to focus on, the actual representation of the project is different compared to preproduction materials.

Reflections:

The final edit has included motion graphics, fixed audio and corrected colors and it has basically represented our final ideas regarding this projects. The changes I have suggested in the rough cut review have been included and realized. There should be some changes regarding the choice of fonts and illustration style in motion graphics. I met some challenges such as thinking the idea of the motion graphics sequence because it should be more creative to match the voice-over and the transition in each scene. Overall i have been investing my time and utilize my skills to make the perfect contribution to the final results of the project.

Humanity InDependence

Interactive Visual Installation

Descriptions and Process

Humanity In-Dependence consists of a single screen projection of an image that can change based off of audience interaction.

The audience can interact with the artwork by shining a flashlight at the light sensor located inside the grey box container. Upon detecting the flashlight, the screen will switch back and forth between that of a human face and a robotic face. The challenge I met was the projected image was initially a static image. I should make the image to be more dynamic prior to the prompt of the flashlight so that the artwork is less static when seen from a distance. To solve the problem, I re-programmed in the processing of the image. so that it would fade back and forth between the two images via a timer.The audience can continue manipulate the image for as long as they have the flashlight aimed at the light sensor or until they decide to leave. The project requires a person to input a light source in reality which will manipulate the digital space through a computer. I found that the primary visual feedback given by digital image manipulation requires both human and machine working together for a successful interaction to take place.
